Fauna

The landscape of ITB is home to a vast array of animals. Some of these animals include pheasants, rabbits and a vast array of birds. There is evidence all over campus of wildlife such as burrows, badger fur and trails. ITB attracts a lot of bee's in the summer due to the vast amount of ivy that can be found on campus. Ivy provides food for bee's. There is evidence of burrows suggesting badgers and rabbits. Rabbits like to graze on low cut graze so ITB proves perfect for this. Birds such as Rooke's, pigeon's and blackbirds. ITB's complex terrain attracts many species of bird.
The man made pond at the entrance of ITB is home to many different species such as ducks and swans. Herring can be found swimming in the water along with beautiful ducks. You will also find swans both in the water and out of the water. The pond is a hub of wildlife activity, there is always something going on, no matter the weather. The pond adds to the scenic landcape of ITB.
